Research Experiences

Safe Reinforcement Learning

Indian Institute of Technology, Madras

As a part of my dual degree thesis, I am working on building Human-in-the-loop, Safe and Verifiable Reinforcement Learning. I implemented a modified version of the Deep Deterministic Policy Gradients (DDPG) algorithm in the inverted pendulum environment with continuous action masking and achieved 100% safety during exploration, with high safety guarantees of 95% in testing when the safety layer was removed.

Reactive Obstacle Avoidance using CBFs

Royal Institute of Technology, Sweden

In this work, we aim to construct a low level obstacle avoidance controller that does not rely on global localisation or pre-existing maps while providing provable guarantees to safety in an unknown and uncertain environment.

I built a fully functioning pipeline in ROS that generates the safe sets from the depth images and a control barrier function (CBF) based controller that uses these safe sets to generate the velocity commands. After testing with real drones, we aim to submit our work to IROS 2024.

Causal Artificial Intelligence | Patent*

Adobe Research, Bengaluru

With counterparts from other IITs (Indian Institute of Technology), I worked on a group project in the field of Causal Artificial Intelligence. We built a novel deep learning-based causal inference algorithm designed to cluster markets.

*We developed a patent from our work which has been validated by Adobe's Internal Committee and submitted to the US Patent and Trademark Office (USPTO).

Deep RL in Autonomous Cars

Hokkaido University, Japan

I worked on applying deep reinforcement learning in autonomous cars to optimize traffic flow in scenarios like signal intersections and highway merges. I explored various state space encodings for traffic representation and implemented extensions to deep Q learning, such as double Q learning, prioritized replay, dueling networks, and noisy nets.

Having conducted a thorough empirical analysis, I showed that RL-based autonomous cars can reduce congestion time significantly compared to rule-based agents by 30%.
